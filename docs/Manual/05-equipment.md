### Weapon Properties

| Propiedad | Descripción |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Ammunition** | You can only make ranged attacks with ammunition you have. Drawing ammunition is part of the attack. After a fight, spend 1 minute to recover half the ammunition (round down); rest is lost. |
| **Finesse** | When making an attack with a Finesse weapon, use your choice of Strength or Dexterity modifier for attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both. |
| **Heavy** | You have Disadvantage on attack rolls with a Heavy weapon if your Strength isn't at least 13 (Melee) or Dexterity isn't at least 13 (Ranged). |
| **Light** | When you take the Attack action and attack with a Light weapon, you can make one extra attack as a Bonus Action later on the same turn with a different Light weapon. You don't add your ability modifier to the extra attack's damage unless that modifier is negative. |
| **Loading** | You can fire only one piece of ammunition from a Loading weapon when you use an action, Bonus Action, or Reaction, regardless of the number of attacks you can normally make. |
| **Range** | A Range weapon has a range in parentheses after the Ammunition or Thrown property. The first number is normal range, the second is long range. Beyond normal range, you have Disadvantage. You can't attack beyond long range. |
| **Reach** | A Reach weapon adds 5 feet to your reach when attacking with it, and when determining your reach for Opportunity Attacks. |
| **Thrown** | If a weapon has the Thrown property, you can throw it to make a ranged attack, and you can draw that weapon as part of the attack. If Melee, use the same ability modifier for attack and damage rolls. |
| **Two-Handed** | A Two-Handed weapon requires two hands when you attack with it. |
| **Versatile** | A Versatile weapon can be used with one or two hands. A damage value in parentheses appears with the property. The weapon deals that damage when used with two hands. |

### Mastery Properties

Mastery properties are usable only by characters with features (like Weapon Mastery) that unlock them.

| Propiedad | Descripción |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Cleave** | If you hit a creature with a melee attack roll using this weapon, you can make a melee attack roll against a second creature within 5 feet of the first that is also within your reach. On a hit, the second creature takes the weapon's damage, but don't add your ability modifier unless negative. You can make this extra attack only once per turn. |
| **Graze** | If your attack roll with this weapon misses a creature, you can deal damage to that creature equal to the ability modifier you used. This damage is the same type dealt by the weapon, and damage can be increased only by increasing the ability modifier. |

| **Sap** | If you hit a creature with this weapon, that creature has Disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn. |
| **Slow** | If you hit a creature with this weapon and deal damage to it, you can reduce its Speed by 10 feet until the start of your next turn. If the creature is hit more than once by weapons with this property, the Speed reduction doesn't exceed 10 feet. |

### Improvised Weapons

If you use an object (such as a table leg, frying pan, or bottle) as a makeshift weapon, or wield a weapon in an unusual way (such as using a Ranged weapon to make a melee attack), it counts as an improvised weapon.

**Proficiency:** Don't add your Proficiency Bonus to attack rolls with an improvised weapon.

**Damage:** On a hit, the weapon deals 1d4 damage of a type the DM thinks is appropriate.

**Range:** If thrown, normal range is 20 feet and long range is 60 feet.

**Weapon Equivalents:** If an improvised weapon resembles a Simple or Martial weapon, the DM may say it functions as that weapon.

## Armor

The Armor table lists the game's main armor types:

- **Category:** Light, Medium, or Heavy (determines donning/doffing time)
- **Armor Class (AC):** Base AC when wearing the armor
- **Strength:** If shown, armor reduces speed by 10 feet unless Strength equals or exceeds the listed score
- **Stealth:** If "Disadvantage" is shown, the wearer has Disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks

### Armor Training

Anyone can don armor or hold a Shield, but only those with training can use them effectively:

- **Light, Medium, or Heavy Armor:** If you wear armor and lack training with it, you have Disadvantage on any D20 Test that involves Strength or Dexterity, and you can't cast spells.

- **Shield:** You gain the Armor Class benefit of a Shield only if you have training with it.

- **One at a Time:** A creature can wear only one suit of armor at a time and wield only one Shield at a time.

### Variant: Equipment Sizes

In most campaigns, you can use or wear any equipment within the bounds of common sense. Using this variant, when adventurers find armor, clothing, and similar items made to be worn, they might need to visit an expert to make the item wearable. The cost for such work is 1d4 × 10 percent of the market price.

## Poison

Given their insidious and deadly nature, poisons are a favorite tool among assassins and evil creatures.

### Poison Types

- **Contact:** Smeared on an object and remains potent until touched or washed off. A creature touching contact poison with exposed skin suffers its effects.
- **Ingested:** A creature must swallow an entire dose to suffer its effects. A partial dose may have a reduced effect.
- **Inhaled:** Poisonous powders and gases take effect when inhaled. The cloud dissipates immediately. Holding one's breath is ineffective.
- **Injury:** Applied as a Bonus Action to a weapon or piece of ammunition. Remains potent until delivered through a wound or washed off.

### Purchasing Poison

In some settings, laws prohibit the possession and use of poison. Characters with criminal contacts might acquire poison easily. Others might need extensive inquiries and bribes.

### Harvesting Poison

A character can attempt to harvest poison from a dead or Incapacitated venomous creature. The effort takes 1d6 minutes, after which the character makes a DC 20 Intelligence (Nature) check using a Poisoner's Kit. Success = 1 dose. Failure by 5 or more = character is subjected to the creature's poison.

## Crafting Non-Magical Items

To craft a nonmagical item, you need tools, raw materials, and time.

### Tools

You must use the required tool to make an item and have proficiency with that tool. Anyone who helps you must also have proficiency with it.

### Raw Materials

You need raw materials worth half the item's purchase cost (round down). The DM determines whether appropriate raw materials are available.

### Time

To determine days (working 8 hours a day), divide the purchase cost in GP by 10 (round a fraction up to a day). Days need not be consecutive.

Characters can combine their efforts to shorten crafting time. Divide the time by the number of characters working on it. Normally, only one other character can assist, but the DM might allow more.

**Example:** Plate Armor (1,500 GP) requires 750 GP raw materials and 150 days (1,500 ÷ 10).
